Who I am

"As soon as I have got
flying to perfection,
I have got a scheme
about a steam engine."
Ada Lovelace

I started in mechanical engineering — control systems, mechatronics, bioengineering — and spent the early part of my career doing what engineers do: solving problems with rigour and precision. What I kept missing was the human connection.

It was the explaining. The translating. The moment in a room when you can see someone's eyes start to glaze, and you have about thirty seconds to find a different story before you lose them entirely.

I've now had nearly a hundred of those moments with professors, researchers, deans and department chairs across nine US states. I've built a $1.8M pipeline from cold, entirely on the strength of listening carefully and telling the right story back.

I learned that the gap between a technical and a non-technical audience isn't about intelligence — it's about story. Give people the right story and the complex thing clicks.

Outside of work: novel in editing (no spoilers), Second City improv alumna, Boskone short story competition finalist, and a cyclist planning her first bikepacking trip. Wish me luck. I graduated as voted-in valedictorian of the University of Toronto Engineering class of 2021.

Developer advocacy, technical communications, and the art of making hard things make sense. That's where I'm headed. Based in Toronto.
